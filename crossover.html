Definition ˅

A crossover, CUV (crossover utility vehicle), or XUV, is an automobile with an increased ride height that is built on a unibody chassis construction shared with passenger cars. Crossovers are often based on a platform shared with a passenger car; as a result, they typically have better interior comfort, a more pliant ride, superior fuel economy, and less off-road capability than truck-based SUVs.

  • Crossovers use a unibody construction (frame and body as a single piece), making them lighter and more car-like to drive.
  • They offer better handling, fuel efficiency, and a smoother ride compared to traditional SUVs, thanks to their car-based roots.
  • While providing an elevated seating position and often all-wheel drive, they are primarily designed for on-road use rather than heavy-duty off-roading.
  • A defining feature of the crossover is its versatile hatchback-style rear, providing easy access to a flexible cargo space.
Standard Styles ˅

Subcompact crossover (B-segment SUV)

Subcompact crossover SUVs are cars based on a supermini (B-segment) passenger car platform.

Subcompact crossovers are also referred to as "B-SUVs", "B-segment SUVs" and "small SUVs". These generally have a length under 4,400 mm (173.2 in) but are larger than "mini crossovers".

Compact crossover (C-segment SUV)

A compact crossover SUV is a crossover SUV based on a C-segment passenger car platform. A compact crossover SUV usually has a length ranging from 4.3 to 4.7 meters (169 to 185 inches). In terms of size, they sit between a subcompact crossover and a mid-size crossover.

They are generally based on the platform of a compact car (also known as C-segment in Europe), while some models may be based on a mid-size car (D-segment) or a B-segment platform.

Mid-size crossover (D-segment SUV)

A mid-size crossover SUV is a class of crossover SUVs that are larger than compact crossover SUVs, but smaller than full-size crossover SUVs. Mid-size crossover SUVs are usually based on the platform of a mid-size passenger car (D-segment).

The majority of the segment are three-row vehicles, roughly substituting minivans or large MPVs, with a length around 4.8 to 5.1 meters (189 to 201 inches). Two-row mid-size crossovers are also popular in the North American market, with a length around 4.7 to 4.9 meters (185 to 193 inches).

Full-size crossover (E-segment SUV)

Full-size crossover SUVs are the largest size of crossovers, and are almost entirely sold in North America. These vehicles are primarily large three-row vehicles built to replace full-size SUVs (which are built on a body-on-frame truck chassis), and full-size minivans, with a length usually over 5.1 meters (201 inches).

Full-size crossovers are typically based on the platforms of full-size cars. They are considered the "E-segment SUV" alongside full-size SUVs in Europe.

Price Range ˅

Crossovers are currently the fastest-growing segment globally, and their prices reflect the wide range of options available:

  • Entry-Level / Subcompact Crossovers: RM 70,000 - RM 120,000. These are highly popular for urban dwellers wanting a raised driving position without the bulk of an SUV. Examples include the Perodua Ativa, Proton X50, and Honda HR-V.
  • Compact / C-Segment Crossovers: RM 120,000 - RM 180,000. Offering more space and comfort, making them perfect family cars. Examples include the Honda CR-V, Mazda CX-5, and Toyota Corolla Cross.
  • Mid-Size / D-Segment Crossovers: RM 180,000 - RM 250,000+. These offer 3-row seating and premium features, ideal for larger families who prefer car-like handling over a bulky MPV or SUV. Examples include the Mazda CX-8 and Hyundai Santa Fe.
Storage Capacity ˅

Crossovers blend the practicality of a hatchback with the spaciousness of an SUV, making them excellent cargo haulers:

  • Flexible Cargo Space: Crossovers feature a hatchback-style rear door, providing a large, wide opening for easy loading. The cargo floor is usually flush with the opening.
  • Fold-Flat Rear Seats: The second (and third, if applicable) rows of seats are designed to fold completely flat, expanding the cargo volume significantly, often doubling the available space.
  • Hidden Storage: Many crossovers feature under-floor storage compartments in the trunk area, perfect for hiding valuables or storing smaller, loose items.
Durability And Reliability ˅

Crossovers are generally very reliable, but it's important to remember they are built like cars, not trucks.

  • On-Road Focus: Their unibody construction is incredibly strong for daily driving and provides excellent crash safety. However, it is not designed to withstand the severe twisting and impact forces of serious off-roading.
  • Car-Based Mechanicals: Crossovers share engines, transmissions, and suspension components with passenger cars. This means they are generally highly reliable and inexpensive to maintain, provided they are used within their intended limits.
  • AWD vs 4WD: Many crossovers offer All-Wheel Drive (AWD) rather than true Four-Wheel Drive (4WD). AWD is excellent for wet roads and light snow, but lacks the low-range gearing needed for rugged off-road trails.
