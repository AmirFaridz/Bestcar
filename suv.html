Definition ˅

An SUV or Sport Utility Vehicle is a car classification that combines elements of road-going passenger cars with features from off-road vehicles, such as raised ground clearance and four-wheel drive. There is no commonly agreed-upon definition of an SUV, and usage of the term varies between countries.

  • High seating position and elevated ground clearance.
  • Roomy interior, often accommodating five to seven or eight passengers.
  • A two-box design, where the engine is in the front compartment and the passenger and cargo areas are combined in the rear compartment.
  • Often built on a light-truck chassis, providing a robust frame.
  • Available in two-wheel-drive or four-wheel-drive/all-wheel-drive configurations.
Standard Styles ˅

Mini SUV

The smallest class of SUV. In Japan, SUVs under 3,400 mm (133.9 in) are classified as kei cars.

Compact SUV

A compact SUV is a class of small SUVs.

Mid-size SUV

A mid-size SUV is a class of SUVs that are sized between compact and full-size SUVs.

Full-size SUV

Full-size SUVs are the largest size of commonly produced SUVs. Some, such as the Ford Expedition and Chevrolet Tahoe, are marketed for their off-road capabilities, and others, such as the Lincoln Navigator and Cadillac Escalade, are marketed as luxury SUVs. Full-size SUVs usually have three rows of seats.

Extended Length SUV

Extended-length SUVs are large SUVs that have a longer wheelbase and overall length. Examples include the Ford Expedition MAX, and the Chevrolet Suburban. The additional length provides more passenger and cargo space.

Coupe SUV

A coupe SUV is a type of SUV with a sloping rear roofline, similar to those of fastbacks or fastback coupes. The sloping roofline is adopted to offer a styling advantage compared to its standard counterpart. The body style is also referred to as an SUV coupe or a crossover coupe.

Price Range ˅

The price of an SUV can vary widely based on the vehicle's size, class, brand, and features. Here is a general breakdown of SUV price ranges:

  • Subcompact / Compact SUVs: RM 60,000 - RM 120,000. These are the most affordable SUVs, offering a good balance of value, fuel efficiency, and utility. Examples: Perodua Ativa, Honda HR-V, Proton X50.
  • Mid-Size SUVs: RM 120,000 - RM 200,000. Offering more space, power, and advanced features, they are highly popular among families. Examples: Honda CR-V, Mazda CX-5, Toyota Harrier.
  • Full-Size / 7-Seater SUVs: RM 200,000 - RM 350,000+. Ideal for larger families, these provide maximum space and comfort. Examples: Toyota Fortuner, Hyundai Santa Fe.
  • Luxury / Premium SUVs: RM 350,000+. Focused on premium materials, cutting-edge tech, top-tier performance, and brand prestige. Examples: BMW X5, Mercedes-Benz GLE, Volvo XC90, and Porsche Cayenne.
Storage Capacity ˅

SUVs are known for their spacious interiors and versatile storage options. The "two-box" design makes them ideal for carrying larger loads.

  • Generous Cargo Space: Compact SUVs typically offer 15 to 30 cubic feet of cargo space behind the second row, while mid-size and full-size models can offer up to 40 to 50 cubic feet or more.
  • Fold-Flat Seats: A major advantage of SUVs is the ability to fold down the rear seats (second and third rows) to create a massive, flat loading floor. This allows them to accommodate very large or awkwardly shaped items.
  • Height Advantage: The taller roofline allows SUVs to carry taller items easily, such as furniture, bicycles, or large appliances.
Durability And Reliability ˅

SUVs are typically built to withstand tougher conditions than standard passenger cars, making them durable options:

  • Rugged Construction: Many SUVs (especially full-size or off-road oriented models) use a body-on-frame construction, similar to trucks, which makes them highly durable and capable of handling rough terrain.
  • All-Weather Capability: The availability of All-Wheel Drive (AWD) or Four-Wheel Drive (4WD) systems enhances traction and stability in adverse weather or off-road conditions, reducing strain on the vehicle.
  • Ease of Maintenance: As popular family and utility vehicles, SUVs benefit from widespread parts availability and knowledgeable mechanics. Regular maintenance ensures they can easily surpass 150,000 to 200,000 miles.
