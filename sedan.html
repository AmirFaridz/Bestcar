Definition ˅

A sedan (/sɪˈdæn/), or saloon, is a passenger car in a three-box configuration with separate compartments for engine, passenger, and cargo. Sedan's first recorded use as a name for a car body was in 1912. The name comes from a 17th-century development of a litter, the sedan chair, a one-person enclosed box with windows and carried by porters.

  • A B-pillar that supports the roof and rear window.
  • Two rows of seats.
  • A three-box design with the engine at the front and the cargo area at the rear.
  • A less steeply sloping roofline than a coupé, which results in greater headroom for rear passengers and a less aerodynamic appearance.
  • A rear interior volume of at least 33 cu ft (0.93 m³).
Standard Styles ˅

Fastback sedans

A fastback sedan is a two-box sedan with a continuous slope from the roof to the base of the decklid, but lacks a hatchback feature. Examples of fastback sedans include the 1930s Stout Scarab, 1940s Tatra, 1950s Ford (UK) Zephyr.

Liftback sedans

Liftback sedans have a fastback profile, but instead of a trunk lid, the entire back of the car lifts up (using a hatch). Examples include the Tesla Model S, Audi RS7, and Kia Stinger.

Notchback sedans

Notchback sedans are a three-box sedan, where the passenger volume is distinctly separate from the trunk volume. The roof is generally flat, with a rear window that slopes sharply down to the car's trunk lid.

Hardtop sedans

Hardtop sedans are a body style of cars without a B-pillar. They were popular in the United States from the 1950s into the 1970s.

Price Range ˅

The price of a sedan car can vary greatly depending on the make, model, features, and whether it's new or used. Here is a general breakdown of sedan price ranges:

  • Subcompact/Compact Sedans: RM 40,000 - RM 90,000. These are highly affordable, fuel-efficient cars that are perfect for daily commuting.
  • Mid-Size Sedans: RM 90,000 - RM 150,000. This is the most popular category, offering a good balance of space, comfort, and features.
  • Full-Size/Large Sedans: RM 150,000 - RM 250,000. These vehicles provide spacious interiors, advanced tech features, and more powerful engines.
  • Luxury/Premium Sedans: RM 250,000+. Luxury sedans focus on high-end materials, cutting-edge technology, top-tier performance, and brand prestige.
Storage Capacity ˅

Sedans are characterized by their "three-box" design, which distinctly separates the engine, passenger cabin, and cargo (trunk) area. This layout impacts their cargo capacity:

  • Trunk Space: Sedans typically have a trunk volume ranging from 12 to 16 cubic feet. While this space is secure and isolated from the passenger cabin, it is generally smaller than what SUVs or hatchbacks offer.
  • Accessibility: The separate trunk provides a secure storage area, hiding valuables from view. However, the trunk opening can be relatively small, making it difficult to load bulky or awkwardly shaped items.
  • Flexibility: Many modern sedans feature fold-down rear seats (often a 60/40 split), which allows for longer items to extend from the trunk into the passenger cabin. This adds a degree of flexibility, though the height of the pass-through is limited.
Durability And Reliability ˅

Sedans are generally recognized for their solid build and longevity, making them a dependable choice for many drivers:

  • Structural Integrity: The lower center of gravity in sedans contributes to better handling and stability on the road. Their aerodynamic shape reduces wind resistance, which can lessen the strain on the engine.
  • Maintenance and Repairs: Sedans often share platforms and components with other models from the same manufacturer, meaning replacement parts are usually readily available and affordable.
  • Engine Performance and Efficiency: The engines in sedans are typically optimized for fuel efficiency and smooth highway cruising rather than heavy towing or off-road use. This focus on consistent, moderate performance helps extend the life of the engine and transmission.
  • Overall Longevity: With regular maintenance, many sedans are known to comfortably exceed 100,000 miles, and models from brands with strong reliability reputations often surpass 200,000 miles with minimal major issues.
