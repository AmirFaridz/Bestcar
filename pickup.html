Definition ˅

A pickup truck or pickup is a light-duty truck that has an enclosed cabin, and an open cargo area with low sides and a tailgate. In Australia and New Zealand, both pickups and coupe utilities are called utes, short for utility vehicle. In South Africa, people of all language groups use the term bakkie, a diminutive of bak, Afrikaans for "bowl" or "container".

  • Once a work or farming tool with few creature comforts, in the 1950s, US consumers began purchasing pickups for lifestyle reasons, and by the 1990s, less than 15 percent of owners reported use in work as the pickup truck's primary purpose.
  • In North America, the pickup is mostly used like a passenger car and accounts for about 18% of total vehicles sold in the United States. Full-sized pickups and SUVs are an important source of revenue for major car manufacturers such as Ford, General Motors, and Stellantis, accounting for more than two-thirds of their global pretax earnings, though they make up just 16% of North American vehicle production.
  • These vehicles have a high profit margin and a high price tag; in 2018, Kelley Blue Book cited an average cost (including optional features) of US$47,174 for a new Ford F-150.
  • The term pickup is of unknown origin. It was used by Studebaker in 1913. By the 1930s, "pick-up" (hyphenated) had become the standard term.
Standard Styles ˅

Mini Pickups

Mini pickups are the smallest class of pickup trucks, and are often based on the platforms of B-segment or C-segment passenger cars, or subcompact or compact crossovers.

Mini pickups are also known as coupe utilities or utes, particularly in Australia and New Zealand.

Compact Pickups

Compact pickups are a class of pickup trucks that are smaller than mid-size pickups. Compact pickups are typically built on a ladder frame chassis, although some models have been built on a unibody chassis.

Compact pickups were introduced to the North American market in the 1960s by Japanese manufacturers, and they quickly became popular due to their low price and good fuel economy.

Mid-size Pickups

Mid-size pickups are a class of pickup trucks that are larger than compact pickups, but smaller than full-size pickups.

The term "mid-size pickup" is primarily used in North America, while in other regions these vehicles are often simply referred to as "pickups" or "1-ton pickups". Mid-size pickups are popular worldwide, and many models are produced globally.

Full-size Pickups

Full-size pickups are a class of pickup trucks that are larger than mid-size pickups. Full-size pickups are primarily sold in North America, where they are extremely popular.

Full-size pickups are typically available in a wide variety of configurations, including different cab styles (regular, extended, and crew cab), bed lengths, and powertrain options.

Price Range ˅

In Malaysia, the pickup truck market is dominated by mid-size (1-ton) models. They range from basic workhorses to premium lifestyle vehicles:

  • Workhorse / Single Cab Models: RM 80,000 - RM 110,000. These are basic models with a single row of seats, a longer bed, and rugged steel wheels. They focus entirely on utility and payload. Examples include the Toyota Hilux Single Cab and Isuzu D-Max Single Cab.
  • Lifestyle / Double Cab (Mid-Spec): RM 110,000 - RM 150,000. The most popular category, offering comfortable seating for 5 passengers and a usable bed. They feature more car-like interiors, alloy wheels, and touchscreen infotainment. Examples include the Ford Ranger XLT, Mitsubishi Triton, and Nissan Navara.
  • Premium / Lifestyle (High-Spec): RM 150,000 - RM 220,000+. These are top-tier models loaded with luxury features, advanced safety systems (ADAS), and powerful engines. They are often used as lifestyle vehicles or status symbols rather than for hauling. Examples include the Ford Ranger Raptor, Toyota Hilux Rogue, and Mitsubishi Triton Athlete.
Storage Capacity ˅

Pickup trucks offer unparalleled cargo hauling capabilities, making them essential for heavy-duty work and extreme utility:

  • Open Cargo Bed: The defining feature is the open cargo bed in the rear. This allows for the transportation of items that are too tall, bulky, or dirty to put inside an SUV or van.
  • Payload Capacity: Mid-size pickup trucks (the standard in Malaysia) are commonly referred to as "1-ton" trucks because they are engineered to carry approximately 1,000 kg (1 tonne) of weight in the bed safely.
  • Towing Capability: In addition to payload, pickups are designed with heavy-duty towing in mind. Most modern mid-size pickups can tow between 3,000 kg to 3,500 kg, making them ideal for pulling large trailers or boats.
  • Bed Versatility: The bed can be customized with various accessories, such as hardtop canopies, roller shutters, or tie-down rails, to protect cargo from the elements and theft.
Durability And Reliability ˅

Pickups are engineered to be tough, with a focus on enduring harsh conditions, heavy loads, and long-term reliability.

  • Body-on-Frame Construction: Unlike passenger cars and most crossovers, pickups use a body-on-frame chassis. The body is bolted onto a separate, heavy-duty steel ladder frame. This provides immense strength and rigidity, essential for carrying heavy loads and traversing rough off-road terrain.
  • Heavy-Duty Suspension: Pickups feature robust suspension systems, typically utilizing leaf springs in the rear. While this can result in a stiffer, bouncier ride when unladen compared to a car, it is crucial for supporting heavy cargo without the rear sagging.
  • Diesel Powertrains: The vast majority of pickups in Malaysia are powered by turbo-diesel engines. Diesel engines are favored for their high torque output at low RPMs (necessary for hauling and off-roading) and their long-term durability and fuel efficiency under load.
  • Off-Road Capability: Most lifestyle and premium pickups are equipped with robust 4-Wheel Drive (4WD) systems, featuring a transfer case with low-range gearing (4L). Combined with high ground clearance and specialized off-road modes, they can tackle mud, rocks, and steep inclines that would stop other vehicles.
