Definition ˅

An electric car, battery electric car, or all-electric car is an automobile that is propelled by one or more electric motors, using only energy stored in batteries. Compared to internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles, electric cars are quieter, have no tailpipe emissions, and lower emissions in general.[1] In the United States and the European Union, as of 2020, the total cost of ownership of recent electric vehicles is cheaper than that of equivalent ICE cars, due to lower fueling and maintenance costs.[2][3]

  • Compared to conventional internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles, electric cars are quieter, have no tailpipe emissions, and lower emissions in general.[1] In the United States and the European Union, as of 2020, the total cost of ownership of recent electric vehicles is cheaper than that of equivalent ICE cars, due to lower fueling and maintenance costs.[2][3]
  • Charging an electric car can be done at a variety of charging stations; these charging stations can be installed in both houses and public areas.[4] The two all-time best-selling electric cars are the Nissan Leaf and the Tesla Model 3.
Energy Efficiency ˅

Internal combustion engines have thermodynamic limits on efficiency, expressed as a fraction of energy used to propel the vehicle compared to energy produced by burning fuel. Petrol engines effectively use only 15% of the fuel energy content to move the vehicle or to power accessories; diesel engines can reach on-board efficiency of 20%, while electric vehicles convert over 77% of the electrical energy from the grid to power at the wheels.[86] This efficiency translates directly into lower fuel costs.

  • Electric motors are more efficient than internal combustion engines in converting energy to driving the vehicle. EV motors are generally over 90% efficient, and even with efficiency losses charging the battery and in the inverter, the overall efficiency is above 70%. In comparison, petrol engines are less than 40% efficient.[87]

  • Tesla was named as a leading electric vehicle manufacturer by market share in 2020. In 2020, Tesla accounted for 18% of global pure electric vehicle sales.[143][144] Other major brands in EV sales include Renault-Nissan-Mitsubishi Alliance, Volkswagen, Hyundai and Kia, and Chinese brands like BYD Auto, SAIC, and BAIC.[145][146]

  • Some countries offer subsidies and tax breaks for EV buyers. In some cases, the subsidies can offset the higher purchase price of EVs, making them cost-competitive with petrol and diesel cars. Examples include the UK, China, and Norway.[148][149][150]
  • The cost of EVs is expected to continue to decrease as battery technology improves and economies of scale are achieved. The cost of lithium-ion battery packs has dropped significantly, from an estimated $1,100 per kWh in 2010 to around $137 per kWh in 2020, enabling more affordable EVs.[151]

Standard Features ˅

Electric cars often feature advancements in styling and interior systems, reflecting their modern, forward-thinking nature. Design is often tailored to maximize efficiency and extend range:

  • Aerodynamic Focus: EV styling often incorporates aerodynamic elements like closed grilles, smooth underbodies, and flush door handles. This minimizes drag to optimize range.
  • Large Infotainment Displays: Most EVs boast massive touchscreen displays as central hubs for controlling not only entertainment but also vehicle settings and charging data.
  • Minimalist Interiors: The lack of a bulky transmission tunnel allows for more spacious, airy, and minimalist cabin layouts.
Price Range (Malaysia) ˅

The EV market in Malaysia is expanding rapidly, with prices spanning from entry-level urban hatchbacks to high-end luxury SUVs:

  • Entry-Level / Urban EVs: RM 100,000 - RM 150,000. These are typically compact hatchbacks or small crossovers designed for daily commuting. Examples include the BYD Dolphin and GWM Ora Good Cat.
  • Mid-Range / C-Segment SUVs: RM 150,000 - RM 250,000. This is the most competitive segment, offering practical range and space for families. Examples include the BYD Atto 3, Tesla Model 3, and Hyundai Kona Electric.
  • Premium SUVs and Sedans: RM 250,000 - RM 400,000+. These offer extended range (often 500km+), premium interiors, and advanced features. Examples include the Tesla Model Y, BMW iX3, and Kia EV6.
  • Luxury / Performance EVs: RM 400,000+. High-end brands offering top-tier performance, luxury, and brand prestige. Examples include the Porsche Taycan, Audi e-tron GT, and Mercedes-Benz EQS.
Storage Capacity ˅

Electric vehicles generally offer excellent packaging and storage, often exceeding their internal combustion engine (ICE) counterparts:

  • The "Frunk" (Front Trunk): Because electric motors are significantly smaller than gas engines, many EVs feature a secondary storage compartment under the hood, providing a secure space for charging cables or smaller bags.
  • Flat Floor: The absence of a transmission tunnel and exhaust system allows for a completely flat interior floor. This creates more legroom for rear passengers and easier interior storage.
  • Flexible Layouts: Because batteries are usually integrated into the floor (a "skateboard" chassis), interior designers have more freedom to create spacious and adaptable cabins.
Durability And Reliability ˅

EVs are often lauded for their lower maintenance needs compared to traditional ICE vehicles.

  • Fewer Moving Parts: An electric motor has a fraction of the moving parts of a combustion engine. There are no oil changes, spark plugs, timing belts, or exhaust systems to maintain or replace, significantly reducing routine service requirements.
  • Brake Longevity: Regenerative braking uses the electric motor to slow the vehicle, recovering energy back into the battery. This dramatically reduces wear on the physical brake pads and rotors, which can last much longer than on conventional cars.
  • Battery Degradation: The main concern for long-term durability is the high-voltage battery pack. While batteries do degrade over time (losing total capacity), modern EV batteries are engineered to last the life of the vehicle, often coming with 8-year/160,000km warranties.
